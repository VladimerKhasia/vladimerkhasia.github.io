Model rivalry based federated learning augmented with geometric learning

Research first questions the two most fundamental concepts in federated learning: 1. the belief that participation is the tool in hand of the individual client device for influencing its own performance gains 2. Knowledge sharing between models via direct weight sharing and weighting scenarios.
In contrast, it is possible to perform local rivalry based distillation between previous and new models, which means that distillation happens for those samples where the new model outperforms the old one. Also instead of clustering and other relative methods from individualized federated learning we use graphs for defusing the benefits towards worst performing models without causing harm for the best performing ones.

Sources of lead exposure in children

As a research consultant at UNICEF I unified previous fragmented, heterogeneous data, conducted initial assessments, used Multiple Indicator Cluster Survey to analyze venous blood specimens covering the children from entire country, analyzed and modeled highly heterogeneous data encompassing the results of stable isotopic ratios of toxic metals, spatial associations, socioeconomic, industrial, environmental, biological information and identified sources responsible for blood lead (Pb) prevalence in children.
